The global carbon footprint average is around 4 tons, but it all depends on where you are.
For example, the average American emits around 16.1 tons of carbon annually and in the
Democratic Republic of Congo, the average person emits only 0.03 tons annually. No matter
how much carbon you as an individual emit, here are some ways to reduce your carbon footprint.

While Traveling

Reduce driving

A typical car produces around 4.6 metric tons of carbon dioxide
annually so going one year without a car can save up to a whole
2.6 tons of carbon dioxide. Of course getting rid of your car is a
personal decision but try to reduce it or substitute using your car
to public transportation such as trains, buses or ride a bike. Try
carpooling when you can. Diving well can also partially reduce
your carbon footprint.

If you want to buy a car, consider buying a cheaper car because
they tend to produce less emissions or consider buying an electric car.

Fly less

Next time you consider going on a vacation abroad, try taking a staycation, however as of now flying is the only way to travel fast far away. Reducing the amount of flights can reduce your carbon footprint significantly.

With your meals

Reduce meat

Cutting meat out of your diet is 800kgs of carbon dioxide out of the
atmosphere annually.This is becauseof the production of meat, a lot
of trees get cut down to create pastures for breeding cows. Cows also
produce methane and animals take up alot of feed and water to farm. “Having
a vegan diet is likely to be the best for the environment, experts say. A vegetarian
or pescetarian diet is also likely to be better for the environment.

Inside your house

Turning off the lights in a room on average can save up to ⅓ kgs of carbon dioxide per day.
Doing other activities such as wearing a sweater instead of turning up the temperature, using
LED lights or hanging clothes on a line and not using a dryer can also reduce your carbon footprint.
Recycling old items and not buying items that are heavily wrapped in plastic.
