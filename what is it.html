The definition of carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gasses
released into the atmosphere because of our actions.

Greenhouse gases

Greenhouse gasses are gasses that trap heat in the Earth’s atmosphere.
There are seven kinds of major greenhouse gasses that are produced by
humans, these include:

  1. Carbon dioxide
  2. Methane
  3. Nitrous oxide
  4. Hydrofluorocarbons
  5. Perfluorocarbons
  6. Sulfur hexafluoride
  7. Nitrogen trifluoride

Effects of greenhouse gasses

The sun shines through the atmosphere during the day, warming the earth's
surface. The earth's surface cools at night, releasing heat into the atmosphere.
However, part of the heat is trapped in the atmosphere by greenhouse gasses.
“According to NOAA's 2020 Annual Climate Report the combined land and
ocean temperature has increased at an average rate of 0.13 degrees Fahrenheit
( 0.08 degrees Celsius) per decade since 1880; however, the average rate of
increase since 1981 (0.18°C / 0.32°F) has been more than twice that rate.”
This causes the earth to overheat. If the earth becomes too hot, many
ecosystems are in danger of undergoing drastic changes that would result
in the extinction of their natural biomes. For example, the number of plants
will drop by half, there will be more heatwaves, and rising sea levels.

  

How are greenhouse gases released

Greenhouse gasses, specifically carbon dioxide, is released when an activity
that needs energy is done for example driving a car or using a television.
This is because most of the energy that is used comes from burning fossil fuels.
