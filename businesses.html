We cannot forget the fact that businesses also contribute to carbon emissions,
manufacturing institutions such as factories have significantly high carbon
emissions. Here are some examples of how businesses can reduce their carbon footprint:

Using green energy

Like I said in the first page, the burning of fossil fuels is what creates
greenhouse gas emissions so if a business switches to using a
renewable energy source such as wind energy or solar power, it will
most definitely reduce the carbon footprint.

Using recycled materials

Instead of using brand new materials to make a product, a business
can use recycled materials to make their products. This is not a
commonly known fact but landfills also have greenhouse emissions,
a lot of emissions actually.

Switch to online meetings and events

Transportation methods such as driving and flying have large carbon
footprints, if a business does only or mostly online events and meetings,
that is reducing their carbon footprint.

Examples of companies that have made efforts to
reduce their carbon footprints

Apple - It has a goal for each apple device that is sold will have zero-net
climate impact. It currently is carbon neutral and the Apple Headquarters
uses solar energy that powers 75% of their energy usage.

Easyjet - Easyjet is an airline. Planes do have really high carbon emissions,
a plane that travels short international flights would be estimated to emit
3.15 grams of carbon for every 1 gram of fuel. Easyjet has made commendable
efforts in trying to reduce their carbon footprint, they did this by implementing
a carbon offset.

BMW - BMW is an automobile company and similarly to Easyjet, makes
commendable efforts to reduce their carbon emissions. “from 2009 to
2019 BMW has been able to reduce its delivery fleet emissions by over
40%” they have started using renewable fuels.
